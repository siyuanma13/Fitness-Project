Definition 1: Progressive Overload

Legend has it that in ancient Greece, a young wrestler carried up a baby calf up a mountain every day for 3 years. As the cow grew, the wrestler grew as well.

While simple, this is the foundation of all strength and physique training--Start off light and gradually improve. Your body will naturally adjust and adapt to the steady increase in load.
1/5

Definition 2: Hypertrophy

Muscle Hypertrophy is the growth of skeletal muscle through the growth the the cells. When we lift weights, we produce microfissures in our skeletal muscle that our body repairs after the workout. This is what causes us to grow. I like to think of bodybuilding as a cycle of creating little holes in the muscle, our body then fills in.

Training and Recovery. Effort and Nutrition. Both of these components are equally important in developing a great physique.

2/5

Definition 3: Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE)

This is how many calories it takes you to get through your normal day. Brushing your teeth, going to work, walking to the post office, etc. all cost a degree of energy, quantified by a number of calories.

To gain muscle, we must be intaking more calories on a daily basis than we need.

As a general rule of thumb, to gain muscle, eat your 1 gram of protein for every 1 lb of bodyweight, and eat 500 calories more than your Total Daily Energy Expenditure.

3/5

Example:

Bob is an average guy who wants to start gaining muscle. He first needs finds his TDE by tracking how many calories he normally eats every day, for 2 weeks.

Assuming his weight doesn't fluctuate during this 2 week period, his TDEE is the average calories/day of those 14 days. Bob adjust his caloric intake so that he now eats 1 gram protein/1 lb bodyweight, and so his total calories is his TDE+500.

TDEE: 2000 calories Bodyweight: 160lbs

Goal Daily Intake: 2500 calories, 160 grams of protein
4/5

That's it. Now all Bob has to do is train.

Non-professional bodybuilding is incredibly simple. Lift weights, eat more than you need, and you will grow.
